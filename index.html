Cuba is ...

Cuba looks like a prince in a poor man's cloak; behind the sometimes dilapidated facades, a golden dust. It is these rich dichotomies that make traveling here exciting. In this country stuck in temporary distortion and shocking economic embargo that lasted more than half a century, you can say goodbye to everyday assumptions and expect surprises.

Historical heritage

Without modern interventions, Cuba's colonial cities have not changed much since as long as musket pirates pursued the Caribbean. The atmosphere and architecture are especially exciting Havana, Trinidad and Camaguey, where grand plazas and cobbled streets tell tales of wealth and intrigues.

Amazing beaches

It doesn't matter if you are staying at a luxury hotel in Havana or exploring the rural charm of Viñales. Don't miss the beach resort town of Varadero, this beachfront paradise should still be seen as part of your Cuban travel plans.
